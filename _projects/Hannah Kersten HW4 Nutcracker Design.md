1. Problem Statement
This proof seeks to develop a simple design for a nutcracker in which grip strength by the average person can feasibly crack a macadamia nut. We will do this by defining the design's dimensions and developing a schematic of the device.

2. Constraints and input parameters
i. Generalized macadamia nut diameter
A common size for a macadamia nut is approximately 25 mm, or about 1 in.

ii. Generalized macadamia nut load to crack
Average load to crack the shell of a macadamia nut is approximately 225 kg/ 2210 N/ 500lbs (Schrauf et al.).

iii. Typical grip strength for a person
For men, this value is around 40kg, and for women, it is around 30 kg. We will generalize this value to 35kg, or about 345N (Leyk et al.)

3. Approach
![Diagram] (/sp26-portfolio-hgk46-ux/assets/images/Nutcracker.jpg)

We want to solve for Lgrip, the length of the device, assuming the grip is at the outermost edge of the nutcracker. This will allow us to complete the scope of the design.

i. Moment balance about point A of the Design
∑M=-Fgrip(Lgrip)+Fm(Lm)=0
Let Lm (distance from end of nutcracker to middle of macadamia nut)= 12.5 mm, which is the radius of a macadamia nut.

We have:
\[
(Lgrip/Lm)=(Fm/Fgrip)=2210N/345N=6.41
\]
Solving for Fgrip, we get 6.41*12.5= 80 mm
Thus, the length of the nutcracker device is 80mm.

4. Discussion on Usability
The nutcracker is usable for most individuals if using their maximum girp strength. The grip is placed at the end of the end of the nutcracker and the nut is able to be cracker in most cases of the adults using the device. The length of the device is just over 3 inches, making it relatively small. This may be convenient for portability of the device.
